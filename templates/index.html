This is the Heirloom Database. Using this software, you may create a digital archive containing photos and descriptions of your important physical objects. Your archive may one day help other people to determine the sentimental, cultural, or monetary value of the items that you document. By recording useful details or anecdotes about the items in your archive, you can preserve information about them that is not apparent by simple examination.

Put simply, if you have a house full of old stuff, then your kids might appreciate a list of what's important and why. That way, they won't throw out something important after you're dead.

To get started with the Heirloom Database, create a new Heirloom by clicking on the button below. Take a photo of the item with your device's camera, write a description, and click submit. This will add the Heirloom to your database. Repeat this process as many times as you like.

When writing the description for an Heirloom, be sure to include all of the details that you think might be relevant. There's no need to be brief. After all, the purpose of this archive is to provide information that other people won't have unless you write it down.

If you want to, you can edit a description for an Heirloom, retake a photo, or delete an Heirloom completely using the buttons on each Heirloom entry. You can also run a simple text search over your archive, which might be useful if you're not sure whether or not you've documented something already.
